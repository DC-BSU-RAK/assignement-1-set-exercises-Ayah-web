Strawberry Cheesecake recipe

Imagine falling inlove with a recipe of creamy and strawberry goodness...
Well, look no further as you stumble across my favorite recipe of a yummy
strawberry cheesecake recipe! This recipe entails a velvety and dreamy cheese-
cake that's as smooth as a cloud, with a sweet-tangy strawberry swirl that dances
through every bite. What about the crust you may ask? It's a buttery, crumbly
nom-nom worthy hug made from beloved graham crackers, holding everything together
like a cozy blanket. To top it all off, is a gorgeous glistening strawberry topping
that hooks you in for the most perfect mouthful! 

        P.s , you might want to hide this after making, otherwise you'll be left with crumbs
        and an empty tummy !

Ingredients are divided into three compononents:

Biscuit Base

  • 200g Graham crackers
  • 120g Unsalted butter

Cheesecake Filling

  • 500g cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cup refined sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 eggs

Strawberry Topping

  • 500g strawberries
  • 2 tsp water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch/cornflour
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare the Biscuit Base

  1. Place the 200g of graham crackers in a
    food processor or a zip-top bag and crush them into fine crumbs.
  2. Melt 120g of unsalted butter and mix it
    with the graham cracker crumbs until it resembles wet sand.
  3. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom
    of a springform pan (9-inch works well). Use the
    back of a spoon or a glass to make it even and compact.
  4. Pop the crust into the fridge to set while you prepare the filling.

Step 2: Make the Cheesecake Filling

  1. Preheat your oven to 160Â°C
  2. In a large bowl, beat 500g of cream cheese
    until smooth and creamy.
  3. Gradually add 1 1/2 cups of refined sugar,
    1 tsp of vanilla extract, and the zest of 1 lemon.
    Mix until well combined.
  4. Add 3 eggs, one at a time, mixing gently
    after each addition. Don't overmix!
  5. Add 1/2 cup of sour cream and 2 tbsp of
    plain flour. Mix until just combined and smooth.
  6. Pour the filling over the chilled biscuit
    base and smooth the top with a spatula.

Step 3: Bake the Cheesecake

  1. To prevent cracks, place the springform
    pan in a larger baking dish and fill the dish with
    hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the pan.
  2. Bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the edges are
    set but the center still has a slight wobble.
  3. Turn off the oven, crack the door open,
    and let the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour.
    Then, remove it and let it cool completely at room
    temperature before refrigerating for at least 4 hours (or overnight).

Step 4: Prepare the Strawberry Topping

  1. In a saucepan, combine 500g of chopped strawberries,
    1/2 cup of white sugar, 2 tbsp of lemon juice, and 2 tsp of water.
    Cook over medium heat until the strawberries soften and release
    their juices (about 5-7 minutes).
  2. In a small bowl, mix 1 1/2 tsp of cornstarch with a
    little water to make a slurry. Add it to the strawberry
    mixture and cook for another 2-3 minutes until thickened.
  3. Stir in 1/2 tsp of vanilla extract, then remove from
    heat and let it cool completely.

Step 5: Assemble the Cheesecake

  1. Once the cheesecake is fully chilled,
    spread the cooled strawberry topping evenly over the surface.
  2. Add fresh strawberry slices or a sprinkle
    of lemon zest for extra cuteness.
  3. Slice, serve (or hide), and enjoy your
    adorable and delicious strawberry cheesecake!
